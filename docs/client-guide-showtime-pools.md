## What Is ServiceOps?

ServiceOps Command Center is your field operations hub — built specifically to handle the part of your business that GoHighLevel (GHL) doesn't: work orders, technician job management, property records, and service documentation.

Think of it this way:

| GoHighLevel handles... | ServiceOps handles... |
|---|---|
| Your customer contacts and CRM | Work orders and job assignments |
| Lead capture and marketing | Property profiles and equipment records |
| SMS, email, and conversations | Technician checklists and job notes |
| Your sales pipeline | Service history per property |
| Booking and calendar | Job completion documentation |

The two systems work together. GHL brings in the customer. ServiceOps runs the job.

---

## How It Connects to Your GoHighLevel

ServiceOps plugs directly into your GHL account through a secure connection called a **webhook**. Here's what that means in practice:

**The automatic flow:**

1. A customer's opportunity moves to the right stage in your GHL pipeline (for example: "Job Booked" or "Service Needed")
2. GHL automatically notifies ServiceOps in real time
3. ServiceOps creates a work order — pre-filled with the customer's name and contact link — ready for you to schedule and assign
4. When a technician marks the job complete, ServiceOps can send a status update back to GHL, keeping your pipeline current without any manual entry

You don't need to copy-paste anything between systems. The connection handles it.

**What you control in GHL stays in GHL.** ServiceOps never touches your customer conversations, SMS automations, or marketing workflows — it only reads and writes what's needed for field operations.

## Creating a Work Order

Work orders can be created two ways: automatically from GHL (described above) or manually by office staff.

**To create a work order manually:**

1. Log in to ServiceOps with your admin account
2. From the left sidebar, click **Work Orders**
3. Click the **New Work Order** button in the top right
4. Fill in the details:
   - **Property** — select the customer's service address
   - **Service Category** — e.g. Weekly Maintenance, Repair, Filter Clean, Heater Service
   - **Priority** — Routine, Urgent, or Emergency
   - **Scheduled Date and Time** — when the job should happen
   - **Assigned Technician** — which tech is doing the job
   - **Description** — any specific instructions for this visit
5. Click **Save** — the work order is now visible to the assigned technician

**Work order status moves through these stages:**

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| New | Created, not yet assigned |
| Assigned | Technician is designated, waiting for the service date |
| In Progress | Technician has started the job |
| Completed | Job is finished, checklist signed off |
| Needs Follow-Up | Technician flagged something requiring office or estimate action |
| Cancelled | Job was cancelled |

You can filter, search, and sort work orders from the main Work Orders list. Status badges make it easy to see what's active, what's overdue, and what needs your attention.

## Property Profiles

Every service address has its own **Property Profile** in ServiceOps. This is where all the important on-site information lives so your techs always have it at their fingertips.

Each property profile stores:

- **Address and access details** — gate codes, parking notes, dog warnings, alarm codes
- **Standing service instructions** — "always backwash before leaving," "leave gate latched"
- **Equipment records** — pump make/model, filter type and size, heater, sanitizer system, automation controller
- **Service history** — a log of every completed work order at that address

You build these profiles once and they save your team time on every visit. No more techs calling the office to ask for the gate code.

## How Technicians Use the Mobile View

ServiceOps is designed for one-handed use on a phone. Your technicians don't need to print anything or carry paper — everything they need is on their screen when they show up to a job.

**What a technician's day looks like:**

**Step 1 — See today's jobs**
When a tech logs in, they land on the **Today's Jobs** screen. It shows only their assigned jobs for today, sorted by scheduled time. Each job card shows the address, service type, and current status.

**Step 2 — Open a job**
Tapping a job opens the job detail screen. From here the tech can see:
- Full property address
- Gate code and access notes (displayed prominently)
- Equipment notes for that property
- The service checklist for this visit

**Step 3 — Complete the checklist**
The checklist is a structured list of steps for the service type (for example, a weekly pool maintenance checklist covers water chemistry, filter check, skimmer baskets, brush and vacuum, equipment visual inspection, and more). Techs tap each item as they complete it.

**Step 4 — Add notes and flag anything unusual**
Techs can type job notes — things like "found hairline crack in filter housing, advised owner" — directly in the job detail. These notes are saved to the property's service history automatically.

**Step 5 — Mark complete or flag for follow-up**
When the job is done:
- If everything is fine, the tech marks the job **Complete** — the work order closes, service history is updated, and GHL is notified.
- If something needs an estimate or office attention, the tech taps **Needs Estimate / Follow-Up** — this flags the work order and can create a follow-up task back in GHL so nothing falls through the cracks.

## Your Dashboard at a Glance

The owner/admin dashboard gives you a live summary of your operations without having to dig through individual records.

From the dashboard you can see:

- **Open work orders** — how many jobs are active right now
- **Today's scheduled jobs** — what's on the books for today
- **Jobs needing follow-up** — anything flagged by a tech that needs your attention
- **Completed jobs this week** — a running count of finished work
- **Overdue work orders** — jobs past their scheduled date that aren't finished yet

The work orders list below the summary cards lets you click into any job for the full detail.

## What's Coming Next

ServiceOps is being built in phases. Here's what's on the roadmap after the current launch:

### Phase 6 — Two-Way GHL Sync
Right now, GHL sends data to ServiceOps automatically. In Phase 6, ServiceOps sends status back to GHL automatically too: job completed → GHL contact updated, estimate flagged → GHL task created for follow-up. This closes the loop so your pipeline stays accurate without any manual updates.

### Phase 7 — Reporting Dashboard
A fuller reporting view with charts and summaries: jobs completed per week, technician productivity, service category breakdown, and overdue job alerts. Designed for the owner to check in a few minutes each morning.

### Phase 8 — Photo Uploads
Technicians will be able to take before/after photos directly in the app. Photos attach to the job and the property's service history, giving you a visual record of every visit.

### Phase 9 — Multi-Account / White-Label
The platform will be expanded so other service companies can use ServiceOps under their own brand. Showtime Pool Service gets the same fully-functional system — this phase just opens it up to others.
